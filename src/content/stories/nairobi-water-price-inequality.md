In Nairobi, water isn't just scarce — it's unequal. Our research for [MajiMap](/majimap/) reveals a stark pricing divide that affects millions of residents every day.

## The Price Gap

Residents connected to Nairobi City Water and Sewerage Company (NCWSC) pay a subsidised rate. But in informal settlements — home to over 60% of Nairobi's population — most residents depend on informal water vendors who charge dramatically more.

The result: **the poorest Nairobians pay the highest price for water**, often for water of unknown quality.

## What's Driving the Inequality

Several factors create this pricing gap:

- **Infrastructure gaps** — NCWSC pipes don't reach most informal settlements
- **Vendor monopolies** — limited competition allows price exploitation
- **No transparency** — residents have no way to compare prices or quality
- **Regulatory gaps** — informal water vendors operate outside quality and pricing oversight

## What MajiMap Will Change

[MajiMap](/majimap/) is designed to make this invisible inequality visible. By mapping water prices, quality, and access points across Nairobi, we create a tool for:

- **Residents** — find the best quality water at the fairest price
- **Advocates** — use data to push for policy change
- **Government** — identify infrastructure investment priorities
- **The MiMaji platform** — connect residents to [verified, fairly-priced water delivery](https://mimaji.co.ke)
